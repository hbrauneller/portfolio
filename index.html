About Me


Originally from Dayton, Ohio, I earned my undergraduate degree at the Ohio State University, and stayed in Columbus, Ohio for about 10 years. I worked in banking during this time; managing bank branches and I also did commercial lending for a bit. My last branch was in a highly hispanic area, and I had many latino staff and customers. As a result, I got to brush up on my spanish and it inspired me to attempt a life-long goal to join the Peace Corps. This appealed to me for many reasons; particularly because I wanted to volunteer and give back, and also I could experience a real adventure in another country and culture while doing it. I ended up serving a little over two years in Guatemala, as a small business advisor to local women's association as my main project. I made lifelong friends, learned an amazing amount, and gained a much deeper appreciation for all we have in our country. For my next step, I enrolled in graduate school at Indiana University in Bloomington. I came back to the US about a month and a half before school started. My area of study was public affairs, with concentrations in nonprofit management and public management. During this time, I met my now husband, an Indy native. After graduating, I got a job at the state and worked my way up through a couple of agencies until my last role, where I worked as the Director of Policy for the Department of Workforce Development. There I learned so much about government programs, policies, and how services funded by federal and state sources are used to serve citizens in different ways. Three years ago, I decided return to the private sector and joined a consulting firm where I have been fortunte to work alongside the President/CEO who has acted as a mentor to me. I really enjoy my job, but I am eager to see how I can apply my interest in coding to increase access to many services and programs that I see through my current work.


My husband and I have two cats: Lou & Ella. We live in downtown Indianapolis and love kayaking in our spare time.
